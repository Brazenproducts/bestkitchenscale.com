How to choose the best meat thermometer

There are a few things worth thinking about before you buy:

  • Instant-read vs. leave-in — Instant-read thermometers are fast and great for spot-checking. Leave-in models stay in the meat during cooking and alert you when done.
  • Speed and accuracy — Look for 1-3 second reads and accuracy within 1 degree Fahrenheit. The Thermapen ONE leads on both counts.
  • Wireless range — If you plan to use a smoker, a Bluetooth or Wi-Fi model like MEATER lets you monitor temperature without standing over the grill.
  • Waterproof rating — Look for IP66 or higher if you plan to use it outdoors or rinse it under water.
  • Number of probes — For smoking multiple cuts simultaneously, a 2-4 probe model like the INKBIRD or Govee saves a lot of hassle.

Frequently asked questions

What is the most accurate meat thermometer?

The ThermoWorks Thermapen ONE is widely regarded as the most accurate consumer meat thermometer available, with accuracy to within 0.5 degrees F and a one-second read time. It is the thermometer food scientists and professional chefs use.

What internal temperature should steak reach?

Rare: 125F, Medium Rare: 130-135F, Medium: 140-145F, Medium Well: 150-155F, Well Done: 160F+. Pull the meat about 5 degrees before your target and rest it — carry-over cooking does the rest.

Can I leave a meat thermometer in the oven?
Only leave-in models like the ThermoPro TP-16 or ThermoWorks ChefAlarm are designed to stay in the meat during cooking. Instant-read models like the Thermapen should only be inserted briefly for a reading.
