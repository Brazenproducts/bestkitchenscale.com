How to choose the best resistance bands

There are four main types of resistance bands, each suited to different uses:

  • Loop bands (mini bands) — Small flat loops like the Fit Simplify set. Best for glute work, physical therapy, and warm-ups. Easy to travel with.
  • Continuous loop bands — Large looped bands like Serious Steel and WODFitters. Used for pull-up assistance, barbell work, and heavy strength training.
  • Tube bands with handles — Like Bodylastics and WHATAFIT. Stackable for adjustable resistance. Good all-around choice for home gym workouts.
  • Flat therapy bands — Like TheraBand. No handles, sold by the foot. The gold standard for physical therapy and upper body rehab.
  • Resistance level — Start lighter than you think you need. Most people overestimate their resistance requirements and buy bands that are too heavy for controlled movement.

Frequently asked questions

What resistance band should a beginner start with?

Start with a light-to-medium resistance. The Fit Simplify set of five is ideal because it gives you multiple options to progress through. For pull-up assistance, start with a heavier band and work down as you get stronger.

Are resistance bands as effective as weights?

Research shows resistance bands produce similar muscle activation to free weights for many exercises. Bands have the advantage of constant tension through the full range of motion, which can be more effective for muscle activation than free weights in certain movements.

How long do resistance bands last?
Quality latex bands from Serious Steel, TheraBand, or WODFitters typically last 1-3 years with regular use. Replace them if you notice cracking, discoloration, or loss of elasticity.
Can I build muscle with resistance bands alone?
Yes. If you progressively overload by using heavier bands or more reps over time, bands can produce significant muscle and strength gains — particularly for beginners and intermediate trainees.
