How to choose the best kitchen scale

Here is what actually matters when picking a kitchen scale:

  • Capacity and precision — Most home bakers need at least 11 pounds capacity. For coffee or spices, 0.1-gram precision matters more than high capacity.
  • Display readability — If you use large bowls, look for a pull-out display like the OXO so you can read the weight without tilting the bowl.
  • Unit switching — A one-button toggle between grams, ounces, and pounds makes recipe following much easier when switching between metric and imperial.
  • Platform surface — Stainless steel platforms are easiest to clean. Avoid plastic platforms if you cook with wet ingredients regularly.
  • Auto-off timing — Some scales shut off after 2-3 minutes, which is annoying mid-recipe. Look for models with longer auto-off timers or a pause feature.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best kitchen scale for baking?

The OXO Good Grips and Escali Primo are the top picks for baking. Both offer 1-gram precision, solid tare functions, and enough capacity for large batches. The OXO's pull-out display is especially useful when working with large mixing bowls.

Do I really need a kitchen scale?

Yes, especially for baking. A cup of flour can vary by up to 30% depending on how you scoop it. Measuring by weight removes that variability entirely and makes recipes much more repeatable.

What precision do I need for coffee?
For pour-over or espresso, 0.1-gram precision is worth having. It makes a noticeable difference in dialing in a recipe. The AWS LB-3000 or Smart Weigh pocket scale are good options.
How do I use the tare function?
Place your empty bowl or container on the scale, press tare/zero, and the display resets to zero. Now add your ingredient and the scale shows only the ingredient weight, not the bowl's.
