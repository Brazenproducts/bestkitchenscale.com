Why You Need a Kitchen Scale

Measuring cups lie. Especially for flour — a cup "scooped" with a measuring cup can hold anywhere from 120g to 170g depending on how packed it is. That 50g difference is why your cookies come out cakey one batch and perfect the next. A kitchen scale removes all that guesswork.

Who Uses Kitchen Scales?

  • Bakers — Grams-based recipes are far more consistent. Every professional bakery uses scales, not cups.
  • Keto and low-carb dieters — Accurately weighing food is the only way to hit macro targets. Eyeballing fat and protein doesn't work.
  • Meal preppers — Weigh once, eat on target all week. Perfect for portioning protein, rice, and vegetables.
  • Coffee enthusiasts — The 1:16 coffee-to-water ratio doesn't work without a scale. Consistent brews require consistent measurements.
  • Anyone following a nutrition plan — Whether it's calorie counting, macros, or portion control, a food scale is your most honest kitchen tool.
💡 Pro tip: Use the tare/zero function to weigh ingredients directly into your mixing bowl. Add each ingredient, tare between additions, and you're done with one bowl and no measuring cups to wash.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best kitchen scale for baking?

The OXO Good Grips is our top pick for baking. Its pull-out display means large mixing bowls don't cover the readout, and its 1g accuracy is exactly what baking recipes demand. If you bake regularly, the $55 price is worth it. Budget option: the Etekcity at ~$12 works great for most home bakers.

What's the best food scale for keto?

The Greater Goods Nourish is purpose-built for keto and macro tracking. It syncs with MyFitnessPal and displays nutritional data directly on the scale's screen. For a simpler approach, the Etekcity's 1g accuracy is more than sufficient for weighing meat, cheese, nuts, and vegetables on a keto plan.

Do I need a kitchen scale that measures in grams?

Yes — especially for baking and nutrition tracking. All six scales on our list measure in grams. Grams are the universal standard in professional kitchens and are required for most European and metric-based recipes.

What capacity kitchen scale do I need?

For most home cooks, 11 lb (5 kg) is plenty. If you bake large batches, make bread dough, or brew beer at home, consider the My Weigh KD8000 with its 8 kg (17.6 lb) capacity. Most keto and macro tracking only requires weighing portions up to 500g, so any scale on our list will handle it.

How accurate does a kitchen scale need to be?

For cooking and baking: 1g accuracy is ideal and is the standard across our top picks. For specialty coffee, espresso, or fine spice blending: look for 0.1g accuracy (some scales offer this as a "precision" mode). The Escali Primo measures to 0.1oz / 1g, which covers both everyday and precision needs.

Are cheap kitchen scales accurate?

The Amazon Basics scale at ~$9 measures accurately to 1g for most everyday tasks — but it's less consistent at very small weights (under 5g) and the build isn't durable. For occasional use, it's fine. For daily cooking, baking, or keto tracking, spending $12–$25 on the Etekcity or Escali is worth the reliability.

What is a tare function on a kitchen scale?

The tare function (sometimes called "zero") resets the display to zero with a container or bowl already on it. This lets you weigh only what you add, not the container. All six scales on our list have a tare function — it's a baseline feature you should require in any kitchen scale.
